Overview

This project uses synthetic Electronic Health Records (EHR) to improve diabetes management. Dashboards were developed in Power BI to identify trends, evaluate treatment plans, monitor costs, and visualize health outcomes across individual and population levels.

Data Cleaning Process

Before building the dashboards, each synthetic dataset was cleaned to ensure consistency and integrity. This included:

  • Removing duplicate patient IDs and visit records
  • Standardizing date formats and units (e.g., weight in kg, blood glucose in mmol/L)
  • Filling missing demographic values using plausible estimates
  • Filtering out patients with incomplete key metrics (HbA1c, BMI, etc.)
  • Creating calculated fields such as BMI Category and Medication Adherence Status

Key Features

  • Monitors medication adherence, blood glucose, BMI, and HbA1c levels
  • Visualizes cost breakdown per patient by city and ethnicity
  • Evaluates patient outcomes and emergency visit trends
  • Filters enabled for gender, age group, ethnicity, and physical activity

This analysis was part of an academic project to explore how data-driven tools can improve healthcare delivery and clinical decision-making for diabetes patients.
